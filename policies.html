Seedling Theatre Company
Policies

Safety Policies

• Students are to listen to and follow the directions and instructions of their teachers and Seedling staff. The rules established at this studio are to keep everyone safe.
• Students are not to touch, pick up, play with, or climb on, any sound or lighting equipment, props, set pieces, ladders, constructions equipment or tools, paint, etc. unless under supervision and with permission of a Seedling instructor. Chairs, desks, set furniture, and tables are not to be stood upon or walked across.
• Absolutely no sitting or climbing on the stage railings is allowed. Students are never to jump off the stage. Students are not to jump off or over any of the stage steps. Students are not to pull on or hang on the stage curtains.
• Running in the studio is prohibited.
• Children who are under the age of 12 must be picked up inside the studio by an authorized parent or guardian. Children over the age of 12 may, with signed permission, walk out to their parent’s car on their own UPON THEIR ARRIVAL. No children are ever to wait outside the studio for their ride to arrive.
• Children will only be released to those individuals who are listed on their Emergency Information sheet. Until our staff learns faces and names, Identification may be requested at pick up.
• Bathrooms at Seedling Theatre Company are single use only unless young children are being assisted by a parent or sibling. Seedling staff members cannot assist students in using the toilet facilities. All students must be able to use the bathroom independently and use the door lock.

Payment and Attendance Policy

Tuition Policies

▪ Tuition is a continuing charge for a monthly rated class or classes.
▪ Tuition payments are due on the 8th day of each month.
▪ Payments not received by the 12th of the month will incur a $15 late fee.
▪ Registration/production fees and tuition payments are non-refundable and non-transferable after the start of the semester classes.
▪ Tuition fees are based on the total number of instruction hours per semester (including dress rehearsals and performances) divided into 6 (fall) or 4 (spring) monthly payments. Regardless of how many classes are held per month, monthly tuition remains at a set rate.
▪ No tuition adjustments or credits are given for missed classes and no make-up classes will be offered, with the exception of studio-cancelled classes.
▪ Classes for the semester end with the final show performance.

Production Fees

All classes that have mid or end-or-semester stage performances are charged a one-time Production Fee. Production fees cover the licensing and royalties for plays and musicals, the acquisition, creation, and maintenance of costumes, sets, and props, scripts, and publicity. Production fees are due at the time of registration in order to reserve your child’s spot in a class.

Payment methods

▪ Starting Fall 2021, all families must be set up for automatic tuition payments through invoices sent to them through SQUARE. ACH payment arrangements are preferred but debit or credit cards may be saved for auto-pay set up as well. Reminder notifications will be emailed on the 1st of each month an accounts will be charged on the 8th of each month.
▪ Registration/Production fees are paid on our website through the online store. Other items such as Show Tickets, promotional packages, and Seedling Credit or gift certificates are also available for purchase online. Front Row Seedling Seats must be purchased in person at our studio.
▪ Manual Tuition Payments: We accept Visa, Master Card, cash, check, and debit cards with a Visa or Master Card logo on them for payment at our studio. A $25 fee will be charged for returned checks.

Withdrawal Policy

Upon class enrollment, students are expected to complete the semester. Shows rely on the commitment of all cast members and participants to work together as a team. Fourteen (14) days written notice is required to discontinue any class. A withdrawal form must be filled out by the parent and submitted to the front desk in person. No refund of prior tuition payments will be issued and any past due payment must be made at the time of official withdrawal. Showcase Cast students are subject to the withdrawal policy outlined in the Cast Agreement. Your account will remain active, until withdrawal procedures have been followed.

Attendance/Tardiness

▪ Consistent attendance is expected. In all our classes, the show rehearsals and educational elements are progressive, working off the previous week’s instruction.
▪ If you know your child will be absent for a class, please give the teacher at least 2 weeks notice if possible. Please call the studio anytime your child is ill or unable to attend class. This is important in planning for rehearsals each week.
▪ No tuition adjustments or credits are given for missed classes and no make-up classes will be offered.
▪ Habitual absenteeism may result in a student’s role in a show being re-assigned to another student.
▪ Classes will begin promptly at the scheduled time. Students are expected to be on time, in proper attire, and prepared for class. Students who are tardy miss important instruction and warm ups and disrupt the class.
▪ Students are expected to take responsibility for learning missed instruction, choreography, directions and blocking and for obtaining any missed information, handouts, etc. We make every effort to post all choreography on our communication app, SLACK. Teachers are always willing to help if asked and classmates can be a great resource as well.

Code of Conduct

At Seedling Theatre Company we respect childhood. We are adults for most of our lives and the world is full of influences that are pushing our kids to grow up too quickly. Our studio is committed to fostering an environment where those influences are checked at the door, where kids feel safe, and where they are not asked to compromise personal values. Our Code of Conduct is an agreement between our staff and you and your children.

Seedling Theatre Company promises to do our best to show respect for our students by:

• Using appropriate language
• Managing student behavior with positivity and patience while being consistent in upholding studio and class rules.
• Selecting only costumes, music, and dramatic materials that are age appropriate
• Keeping lines of communication open and addressing concerns of students and parents
• Maintaining a culture of kindness, support, and encouragement in all our classes

In return, each of our students are expected to:

• Refrain from profanity and crudeness in speech
• Dress appropriately, avoiding short-shorts and skirts, midriff baring tops, strapless or spaghetti strap tops, and anything printed with offensive words or images. If skirts are worn, bike shorts must be worn underneath.
• Keep cell phones put away and out of sight unless specifically allowed by the teacher
• Keep all food out of the theatre and classrooms unless given specific permission
• Treat with care and respect the property of Seedling Theatre Company including microphones and other audio equipment, costumes, stage curtains, sets, props, furniture, and the building in general.
• Refrain from touching Seedling property or wandering into unauthorized areas of the studio without permission by a staff member
• Show respect to teachers and classmates by listening to and following directions
• Speak and treat fellow classmates with kindness and encouragement
• Speak and behave respectfully to their parents while in our studio

Additionally, parents/guardians are asked to please:

• Refrain from profanity and crudeness in speech while in the building
• Abide by the Safety Rules and Regulations and require their children to do the same
• Pick up their children on time from class
• Understand that what their children learn in each class is more important than the role they are given in a show
